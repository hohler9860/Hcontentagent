## FULL SCRIPT (Using Hook A)

### [0:00-0:04] HOOK
**Say:** "Someone you know is wearing a fake watch right now and they have no idea."
**Visual:** Direct to camera, serious expression, tight framing. Green screen shows a super clone Rolex Submariner — the kind that looks flawless to the naked eye.
**Text Overlay:** "THEY DON'T KNOW"
**Energy:** Low, serious. Not joking. This is a different tone than the usual hot takes. Think investigative, not hype.

### [0:04-0:10] THE SCALE OF THE PROBLEM
**Say:** "Super clones have gotten so good that people are spending eight, ten, twelve thousand dollars on marketplace watches that are completely fake. And the community's answer is always the same — just buy from the AD. Cool. Go wait two years for a Submariner."
**Visual:** Green screen shows screenshots of marketplace listings — Chrono24, eBay, Facebook Marketplace — showing Rolex listings in the $8K-$12K range. Then flash a meme or image of someone waiting outside an AD.
**Text Overlay:** "$8K-$12K ON FAKES" then switch to "jUsT bUy FrOm ThE aD" in sarcastic SpongeBob case
**Energy:** Building frustration. The sarcasm on "just buy from the AD" should be unmistakable.

### [0:10-0:20] QUICK TELLS
**Say:** "Here's three things I check every single time. One — the rehaut engraving. On a real Sub, the ROLEX ROLEX ROLEX engraving lines up perfectly at the crown at twelve o'clock. Fakes almost never get this right. Two — the date magnification. Real Cyclops is 2.5x. Most clones are closer to 1.5. Three — weight. A real Submariner is a hundred and fifty-three grams. Super clones are usually ten to fifteen grams off because the steel composition is wrong."
**Visual:** Green screen shows zoomed-in images for each tell: (1) Macro shot of rehaut engraving with arrow pointing to crown alignment, (2) Close-up of Cyclops magnification — real vs fake side by side, (3) Image of a Submariner on a precision scale showing 153g.
**Text Overlay:** "1. REHAUT ALIGNMENT" then "2. CYCLOPS 2.5x vs 1.5x" then "3. WEIGHT: 153g REAL"
**Energy:** Shift into expert mode. Authoritative, rapid-fire, each point delivered crisply. Point at the screen for each tell if using green screen.

### [0:20-0:30] THE SOLUTION
**Say:** "But real talk — most people are never gonna catch this stuff on their own. That's why you work with someone who checks before you buy. Every watch I source gets authenticated before it goes on anybody's wrist. That's the whole point of the concierge model. You shouldn't have to worry about whether your ten thousand dollar watch is real."
**Visual:** Green screen shows behind-the-scenes photos/video of watch authentication process — loupe inspection, caseback verification, movement check under magnification. Then cut to direct-to-camera, no green screen, for the last line.
**Text Overlay:** "AUTHENTICATED BEFORE YOU BUY" then "EVERY. SINGLE. PIECE."
**Energy:** Transition from expert to reassuring. The last two sentences should feel personal and direct — looking right into the camera, honest delivery.

### [0:30-0:35] CTA
**Say:** "If you've ever wondered whether a deal is too good to be true — DM me. I'll tell you for free."
**Visual:** Direct to camera, close-up, slight nod. No green screen.
**Text Overlay:** "DM ME — FREE LEGIT CHECK"
**Energy:** Generous, open. This isn't a hard sell — it's positioning as the trustworthy source. The "for free" should land genuinely.

## FILMING NOTES

- **Camera Setup:** iPhone on tripod, vertical (9:16), tight chest-up framing. Green screen for watch images and comparison graphics. Final CTA segment should be without green screen — just you and a clean background. The shift in visual style reinforces the shift from content to personal appeal.
- **Location:** Home office or neutral background. For the authentication B-roll, shoot separately at a workspace with loupe, watch tools, and a watch on a clean surface.
- **Pacing:** Slower than typical scripts. This is a trust-building video, not a hype video. The quick tells section (0:10-0:20) is the fastest part — rapid-fire expert delivery. Everything else is measured and serious.
- **Music/Sound:** Use "Raindance" or similar atmospheric, slightly darker ambient track. Raindance has 7.9% ER across 2 uses — it works perfectly for more serious, behind-the-scenes content.
- **Wardrobe:** Clean, dark, simple. This is serious content — no flashy jewelry or loud patterns. A dark crew neck or henley.
- **Props Needed:** If you have access to authentication tools (loupe, caseback opener, precision scale), film quick B-roll clips of the authentication process. These clips can be reused across multiple trust-building videos. Watch images: need clear real vs. fake comparison images for rehaut, Cyclops, and weight tells.
- **Lighting:** Slightly lower key than other videos. Not dark, but not bright and poppy. The tone should feel like a late-night conversation, not a sales pitch.
- **Editing Notes:** This video has more cuts than typical — it bounces between green screen education and direct-to-camera moments. The transition at 0:30 from green screen to clean background is important — it signals the shift from "here's the problem" to "here's how I solve it." The quick tells section should use quick zoom-ins on the green screen images as each point is made.
