## FULL SCRIPT (Using Hook C)

### [0:00-0:04] HOOK
**Say:** "Six hundred dollars. Flea market in New Jersey. Patek Philippe Calatrava. Real."
**Visual:** Green screen — rapid image sequence: stack of cash/Venmo screenshot, flea market aerial shot, Patek Calatrava dial close-up. Direct to camera between images, delivering each phrase like a news headline.
**Text Overlay:** Words appear in sequence matching the delivery: "$600" > "FLEA MARKET" > "PATEK" > "REAL."
**Energy:** Staccato delivery. Four statements. Each one more surprising than the last. Dead serious face. No smile yet.

### [0:04-0:10] THE STORY
**Say:** "This happens more than you think. Estate sales, flea markets, pawn shops — people die, families don't know what they have, and a twenty thousand dollar watch ends up on a folding table between a lamp and a cassette player."
**Visual:** Green screen shows images of estate sales and pawn shop watch cases — the unglamorous reality of where luxury watches end up. Quick cuts between 3-4 different images of watches in unexpected settings.
**Text Overlay:** "ESTATE SALES. PAWN SHOPS. FLEA MARKETS."
**Energy:** Shift from staccato to storytelling. Paint the picture. The "between a lamp and a cassette player" line should be delivered with a slight smirk — the absurdity is the point.

### [0:10-0:18] THE GREY MARKET ANGLE
**Say:** "The grey market isn't just online dealers in a warehouse somewhere. It's anywhere a watch trades hands outside the authorized channel. And some of the best deals I've ever seen came from places nobody thinks to look. A vintage Omega from an estate in Connecticut — eight hundred bucks. A Cartier Tank from a pawn shop in Miami — twelve hundred."
**Visual:** Green screen shows each example as it's mentioned: vintage Omega Seamaster (estate auction lot photo), Cartier Tank (pawn shop display case). Real-looking images, not polished marketing shots — the rawness is the point.
**Text Overlay:** "OMEGA — $800 ESTATE SALE" then "CARTIER TANK — $1,200 PAWN SHOP"
**Energy:** Excited, genuine. Like you're sharing insider stories with a friend over drinks. Lean into the storytelling.

### [0:18-0:25] THE CATCH
**Say:** "But here's the thing — for every real Patek at a flea market, there's fifty fakes. You need to know what you're looking at. Or you need to know someone who does."
**Visual:** Green screen shows a split image: genuine Patek vs. obvious fake side by side (different quality, wrong details visible). Then cut to direct-to-camera, no green screen. Knowing look.
**Text Overlay:** "50 FAKES FOR EVERY REAL ONE"
**Energy:** Slow down here. Get serious. This is the pivot from fun storytelling to real advice.

### [0:25-0:30] CTA
**Say:** "If you find something wild out in the world, send me a pic before you buy it. I've seen it all. Worst case I save you from a fake. Best case I help you score a Patek for six hundred bucks."
**Visual:** Direct to camera, close-up, relaxed smile. No green screen — just you.
**Text Overlay:** "SEND ME THE PIC" with DM arrow pointing to profile
**Energy:** Warm, approachable. The CTA is genuinely helpful, not salesy. End with a slight laugh or head shake — the "six hundred dollar Patek" absurdity is inherently funny.

## FILMING NOTES

- **Camera Setup:** iPhone on tripod, vertical (9:16), chest-up framing. Green screen for all images. Direct-to-camera for the close (0:25-0:30) — drop the green screen and let the clean background signal authenticity.
- **Location:** Home office or neutral space. The green screen does the heavy lifting on visuals.
- **Pacing:** This is a STORY, not a list. The pacing follows a narrative arc: rapid-fire hook > slow storytelling > building excitement > serious pivot > warm close. The variety in pacing keeps the viewer locked in.
- **Music/Sound:** Use "float" or similar ambient track. The storytelling format benefits from something atmospheric that doesn't compete with the voice. Float has proven performance (11,796 avg plays across 3 uses).
- **Wardrobe:** Casual, approachable. This video's energy is "your friend telling you a crazy story" — dress the part. T-shirt, maybe a flannel. Nothing that says "watch dealer."
- **Props Needed:** Phone with images loaded for green screen: flea market scenes, estate sale watch photos, pawn shop displays, Patek Calatrava hero shot, vintage Omega, Cartier Tank. Real-looking/raw photos are better than polished marketing images here — the authenticity of the setting is what sells the story.
- **Lighting:** Natural, warm. This is a storytelling video — it should feel like golden hour, not a studio shoot.
- **Editing Notes:** The hook is the most critical edit. The four rapid-fire images at 0:00-0:04 need to hit in perfect sync with the four spoken phrases. Each image appears for about one second, timed to the word. The rest of the video is more conventional green screen cuts. The transition at 0:25 from green screen to direct-to-camera is the trust signal.
