## FULL SCRIPT (Using Hook A)

### [0:00-0:04] HOOK
**Say:** "You think your Daytona costs fifteen grand? Nah. Do the math. It's closer to fifty."
**Visual:** Direct to camera, tight framing. Green screen shows a hero shot of the Rolex Daytona — white dial, steel, the one everyone wants.
**Text Overlay:** "THE REAL COST" — bold, center screen
**Energy:** Dead serious. Not yelling, not joking. Flat delivery like you're breaking bad news to a friend.

### [0:04-0:10] THE AD GAME EXPLAINED
**Say:** "To even get offered a Daytona at an AD, most dealers want you to build a purchase history. We're talking thirty to fifty thousand in jewelry, watches you didn't want, stuff your wife picks out just to keep the relationship going."
**Visual:** Green screen shows a running tally graphic building up: "$8K bracelet... $12K earrings... $15K Datejust you didn't want..." Items stack up with dollar amounts.
**Text Overlay:** Numbers stacking: "$8K + $12K + $15K + $7K + $8K = $50K+" — each line appearing as he mentions spending categories
**Energy:** Slight disbelief in the voice, like you're recounting something absurd. Casual hand gestures.

### [0:10-0:18] THE REAL MATH
**Say:** "So you spent fifty grand to earn the right to spend another fifteen on the Daytona. That's sixty-five thousand dollars. Oh, and you waited eighteen months. You know what you could've done instead?"
**Visual:** Green screen switches to a simple math breakdown graphic: "$50K purchase history + $15,100 Daytona = $65,100 TOTAL COST" then "18 MONTHS WAITING" appears below. Clean, bold typography.
**Text Overlay:** "$65,100 REAL COST" in large font, "18 MONTHS" below it
**Energy:** Building momentum. Speed up slightly. The math should hit like a punchline.

### [0:18-0:27] THE GREY MARKET ANSWER
**Say:** "Walked into a grey market dealer, paid twenty-five grand for the exact same Daytona, had it on your wrist the same day. No waitlist. No purchase history. No games. You just saved forty thousand dollars and a year and a half of your life."
**Visual:** Green screen shows side-by-side comparison: LEFT: "AD ROUTE — $65K total, 18 months, purchase history required" | RIGHT: "GREY MARKET — $25K, same day, walk in walk out." The grey market side has a green checkmark, AD side has a red X.
**Text Overlay:** "AD: $65K + 18 MONTHS" vs "GREY: $25K + SAME DAY" — the comparison should be stark and clean
**Energy:** This is the payoff. Deliver it like a mic drop. Confident. "Same day" should land hard.

### [0:27-0:33] CTA
**Say:** "Stop playing the AD game. The math doesn't work. DM me if you want real Daytona pricing."
**Visual:** Cut to direct-to-camera, no green screen. Close-up, looking right into the lens. Small knowing nod.
**Text Overlay:** "DM FOR DAYTONA PRICING" with an arrow pointing to profile
**Energy:** Calm, definitive. Like the conversation is over because the facts speak for themselves.

## FILMING NOTES

- **Camera Setup:** iPhone on tripod, vertical (9:16), tight chest-up framing. Green screen effect for watch images and math graphics.
- **Location:** Clean home office or neutral background. Green screen handles everything.
- **Pacing:** This is a slightly longer script (32-38s) but justified by the math breakdown. Every second has information. No filler. The pacing should feel like someone building a case — steady acceleration to the comparison payoff.
- **Music/Sound:** Use "float" (ambient/downtempo). Proven performer at 11,796 avg plays. Keep it subtle — this video lives and dies on the voice delivery and the math visuals.
- **Wardrobe:** Dark tee or henley. Clean, not flashy. The watches are the flex, not the outfit.
- **Props Needed:** Phone with images loaded: Rolex Daytona hero shots, math/comparison graphics (can be made in Canva — keep them clean, bold numbers, minimal design). If you own or have access to a Daytona, flash it at the CTA for a flex.
- **Lighting:** Even, slightly moody. Not too bright — the seriousness of the topic benefits from slightly lower-key lighting.
- **Editing Notes:** The math graphics are the backbone. They need to appear in sync with the voiceover — each number pops as it's spoken. The side-by-side comparison at 0:18 is the money shot. Make it clean, make it readable, make it undeniable.
