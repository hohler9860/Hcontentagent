## FULL SCRIPT (Using Hook B)

### [0:00-0:03] HOOK — THE SETUP
**Say:** Nothing. Silence. Let the text and facial expression do the work.
**Visual:** Camera at eye level. Creator is standing, looking down at something off-screen (the friend's wrist). Slowly raises eyes to look directly into camera with a "oh no" expression. Slight jaw clench. Eyes widen just barely.
**Text Overlay:** "POV: he asks you to legit check his new Submariner and you already know"
**Energy:** Completely silent. The humor comes from the facial reaction. This is the scroll-stopper — the viewer sees the POV text and the expression and immediately wants to see what happens.

### [0:03-0:06] THE INSPECTION — INTERNAL MONOLOGUE
**Say:** *[Voiceover, internal monologue style — not spoken out loud to the "friend"]:* "Alright... bezel looks... okay. Cyclops is a little small though. And that rehaut... yeah that rehaut is NOT it."
**Visual:** Close-up shots of a watch (or green screen images of a Submariner) — camera slowly panning across the bezel, then the Cyclops, then the rehaut engraving. Each shot holds for 1-1.5 seconds. Creator's hand comes into frame, tilting the watch slightly.
**Text Overlay:** "bezel... ok" then "cyclops... hm" then "rehaut... NAH" — text appears in sync with the voiceover, getting more alarming
**Energy:** Internal monologue voice — quieter, more measured, like thinking out loud. The "yeah that rehaut is NOT it" has finality.

### [0:06-0:10] THE FACE — EXTERNAL REACTION
**Say:** *[Out loud, to the "friend"]:* "It's... nice bro. Where'd you get it again?"
**Visual:** Cut back to the wide shot from the hook. Creator is looking at the "friend" (off-camera to the right) with a forced smile. The smile doesn't reach the eyes. One hand comes up to scratch the back of the neck — universal body language for uncomfortable.
**Text Overlay:** "it's nice bro" — in a slightly different, shakier font to emphasize the lie
**Energy:** ACTING MOMENT. The comedy is in the gap between what he knows (it's fake) and what he says (it's nice). The forced smile is everything.

### [0:10-0:15] THE ASIDE — BACK TO CAMERA
**Say:** *[Voiceover, internal monologue]:* "He paid nine grand for this. On Facebook. With no papers. I can't... I can't tell him."
**Visual:** Creator slowly turns back to camera. The "friend" is now off-screen. Creator's expression shifts from forced smile to dead-eyed stare directly into the lens. Small head shake.
**Text Overlay:** "$9,000. Facebook. No papers." — each phrase appearing on a separate line, building the horror
**Energy:** The comedy peaks here. The slow turn to camera, the dead stare, the head shake — this is the "Jim from The Office" moment.

### [0:15-0:20] THE PIVOT — REAL TALK
**Say:** *[Direct to camera, breaking the skit]:* "This is why you work with someone who checks before you buy. Not after. Every watch I source comes authenticated. No guessing. No praying."
**Visual:** Hard cut — different angle or slightly different framing to signal the skit is over and this is real talk. Creator is now speaking directly to the viewer, not in character. Clean background, no green screen.
**Text Overlay:** "AUTHENTICATE BEFORE YOU BUY" then "NOT AFTER"
**Energy:** Shift from comedy to genuine. The tonal change is jarring on purpose — it's the "but seriously" moment. Quick, confident delivery. Not preachy, just factual.

### [0:20-0:23] CTA
**Say:** "Tag your friend who needs this."
**Visual:** Creator points at camera, slight smile returning. Quick.
**Text Overlay:** "TAG HIM" with arrow
**Energy:** Light, quick. The CTA brings the energy back up — it's a callback to the comedy. The "your friend" mirrors the skit's "friend" character.

## FILMING NOTES

- **Camera Setup:** iPhone on tripod, vertical (9:16). Two setups needed: (1) Wide shot, eye-level, for the POV reaction shots and "talking to friend" moments, (2) Slightly tighter framing, different angle or height, for the direct-to-camera real talk at the end. The angle change signals the break between skit and advice.
- **Location:** Living room, kitchen, or any casual space where two friends would be hanging out. NOT an office — this needs to feel like a real social moment. Counter with some drinks in the background, couch area, etc.
- **Pacing:** This is the fastest-paced video in the batch. Quick cuts. The skit portion (0:00-0:15) should feel like a TikTok comedy — rapid editing, tight cuts, no wasted frames. The real-talk portion (0:15-0:23) slows down just slightly for contrast.
- **Music/Sound:** Use a trending TikTok sound that fits POV/comedy format. Alternatively, use a dramatic suspense sound effect during the inspection montage (0:03-0:06), then silence for the "it's nice bro" moment, then a subtle ambient track for the real talk. The concept brief says this one gets original audio or trending sound — original audio with sound effects is the move.
- **Wardrobe:** Ultra casual. T-shirt, hoodie, or crewneck. This is "at a friend's place" energy. If wearing a watch, make it subtle — don't upstage the "friend's" watch.
- **Props Needed:** A watch to use as the "friend's Rolex" for the close-up inspection shots. It doesn't need to be a Submariner — you can use green screen for the close-ups or just show your own wrist and let editing do the work. The close-up shots (bezel, Cyclops, rehaut) can be pulled from online images and shown on green screen during the internal monologue.
- **Lighting:** Natural, casual. Whatever matches the hangout location. Not styled — the authenticity of the setting is what makes POV content work.
- **Acting Notes:** THIS VIDEO LIVES AND DIES ON THE FACIAL EXPRESSIONS. Practice the "oh no" look in the hook. Practice the forced smile at 0:06. Practice the dead-eyed turn to camera at 0:10. If the acting doesn't land, the joke doesn't land. Film multiple takes and pick the best reactions.
- **Editing Notes:** Jump cuts between the close-up watch inspection and the face reactions. The internal monologue voiceover should be recorded separately and laid over the watch close-ups — it shouldn't sound like it's being spoken in the room. Add a subtle sound design moment (record scratch, suspense sting, or silence drop) at the "NAH" moment during the rehaut check. The hard cut at 0:15 from skit to real talk needs to be ABRUPT — different framing, different energy, possibly a different background or at minimum a different camera angle.
