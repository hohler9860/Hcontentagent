### The Big Picture: Why Grey Market Prices Are Compressing

Here's what's happening and why it matters to you.

The pandemic watch bubble has fully deflated. The speculative buyers who were flipping Daytonas and Nautiluses for 2x retail in 2022? They're gone. What's left is actual demand from actual watch enthusiasts. And actual demand is rational -- it responds to value.

The result: grey market premiums on mainstream references have narrowed significantly. For a lot of models, the "grey market tax" that everyone warned you about has flipped into a grey market discount. You're saving money by going grey. That was not the case 18 months ago.

For you, this means one thing: **this is the best buying window for grey market watches since before the pandemic.** And it's about to get even more interesting once Watches & Wonders shakes up the lineup.

### 5 Watches That Will DROP After Watches & Wonders

Every year, the same thing happens. Brands announce new models at Watches & Wonders. The outgoing generation immediately dips on the grey market. People who were holding out for the "current" model suddenly want the "new" model, and the previous reference floods the secondary market.

Here are 5 references I expect to dip in the next 30-60 days:

**1. Rolex Submariner 124060 / 126610LN**
The rumors about a Submariner update are loud. If Rolex touches the Sub at W&W, the current 41mm references will see a significant correction. The no-date 124060 is already at or below retail on grey -- it could dip another $500-$1,000 post-announcement. If you want one, wait three weeks.

**2. Omega Speedmaster Moonwatch (Current Ref)**
Omega has been refreshing the Speedmaster line incrementally. Any new dial options or movement upgrades announced at W&W push the current configuration down. Already trading at $6,600-$7,200 -- could dip to $6,000-$6,500. The Moonwatch under $6,500 is a generational buy.

**3. Tudor Ranger**
Already below retail on grey. A Ranger refresh (rumored) pushes the current generation under $2,000. At that price, you're getting a COSC-certified, in-house movement watch from a Rolex-owned brand for less than an Apple Watch Ultra. Think about that.

**4. JLC Reverso (Current Generation)**
JLC has been hinting at new dial options and limited editions. Every time they release something new in the Reverso line, the standard production models dip 10-15% on grey as collectors rotate into the new releases.

**5. AP Royal Oak 15500ST**
If AP announces a successor or significant update to the 15500, this reference corrects hard. We're talking a potential $5,000-$10,000 swing on grey. The 15500 is currently trading at $28K-$32K -- a new generation announcement could pull it down to $22K-$26K range.

**1. Outgoing Rolex references (IF confirmed discontinued)**
If the Submariner 124060 gets replaced, the people who want the "last 124060" will pay a temporary premium for new-old-stock examples. It's irrational, but it happens every single time. If you're sitting on one, hold it through the announcement.
