## Content Formula — What Makes dula.mov Work

### 1. Ultra-Short, Punchy Captions
His captions are 2-5 words max. No full sentences. No explanations. Just vibes:
- "Tool watches" (372K plays)
- "Different dials" (307K plays)
- "Rolex bracelets" (122K plays)
- "Crazy times" (24K plays)
- "Stop" (46K plays)
- "Not valid" (11K plays)
- "Thug" (7K plays)

**Pattern:** The caption is a TEASER, not a description. It creates curiosity by being intentionally incomplete or cryptic.

### 2. Green Screen / Commentary Format
- Shows watch images/videos on green screen
- Talks directly to camera over the content
- Casual, off-the-cuff commentary style
- Feels like he's reacting to the content in real-time, not reading a script

### 3. Rolex Dominance
- Nearly every video features Rolex in hashtags or content
- Smart: Rolex is the #1 search term in watch TikTok
- Specific models get called out: Submariner, Datejust, GMT, Skydweller, Milgauss

### 4. Original Sound (Own Voice)
- Uses his own voice/commentary, not trending sounds
- This works because his personality IS the content
- The audio is him being opinionated, casual, and entertaining

### 5. Opinion-Driven, Not Educational
- He's not teaching — he's reacting, ranking, judging
- "Best and worst Skydweller" — not "Everything about the Skydweller"
- "Stop engraving the back of your watch" — direct command, strong opinion
- "Stop" — literally just a one-word hot take caption

### 6. Incomplete / Curiosity Captions
- "Do we wanna see it" — see WHAT? Forces you to watch
- "Buy the Rolex g" — casual, incomplete, makes you curious
- "Need stop this now" — grammatically loose, casual, urgent
- "Bruce Wayne in year is crazy" — cultural reference + incomplete thought

### 7. Engagement Bait Through Controversy
- "Stop engraving the back of your watch" — divisive opinion (42 comments)
- "Stop" — provokes "stop what??" curiosity (76 comments)
- "Not valid" — dismissive hot take (13 comments)

## How to Adapt dula.mov's Style for Dialed by H

### Keep:
- **Ultra-short captions** — 2-5 words, cryptic, curiosity-driven
- **Green screen format** — show watches while talking over them
- **Strong opinions** — take a stance, don't hedge
- **Casual delivery** — sounds like talking to a friend, not a camera
- **Rolex as anchor topic** — it's the algorithm magnet
- **Incomplete thoughts** — force people to watch to understand
- **Fast pacing** — get to the point, no intros

### Adapt:
- **Add grey market angle** — dula.mov doesn't do sourcing/pricing; that's our differentiator
- **Slightly more educational** — weave in "here's how to actually buy this" after the hot take
- **CTA for business** — dula.mov is pure content; we need DM/follow conversion hooks
- **Broader brand coverage** — add Patek, AP, Omega alongside Rolex
- **Specific pricing** — dula.mov speaks in vibes; we speak in dollars (that's our value-add)

### Don't:
- Don't lose the casual energy by over-scripting
- Don't write 3-sentence captions — keep them SHORT
- Don't explain the joke/take in the caption — let the video do the work
- Don't add "Episode X" or series framing — every video stands alone
